The problem

Every autonomous fleet shares the same bottleneck: energy.

Millions of robots already operate around the world. They move packages, deliver food, inspect infrastructure, survey farmland, and explore the Moon. But every single one eventually stops. They run out of battery, sit idle for hours, or need a human to plug them in. Energy is the invisible ceiling holding autonomous fleets back.

Robots are already here

Space
Lunar & Planetary Rovers

On the Moon, energy is life or death. Rovers face extreme cold, two-week nights without sunlight, and no way to recharge. Multiple missions ended because the rover simply ran out of power.

[ Photo: sidewalk delivery robot ]
Last Mile
Delivery Robots

Small sidewalk robots delivering food, groceries, and packages. Starship and Serve Robotics already run fleets across U.S. and European cities. They recharge constantly between runs.

[ Photo: humanoid robot ]
Humanoid
Humanoid Robots

Tesla Optimus, Figure, Apptronik. Human-shaped robots built for factories, hospitals, and homes. They'll need constant power to match a human work shift.

[ Photo: warehouse robots ]
AMR
Autonomous Mobile Robots

The backbone of modern logistics. Amazon, DHL, and Walmart deploy thousands of AMRs to move inventory 24/7. They navigate on their own, coordinate in fleets, and never take breaks. Except to charge.

[ Photo: delivery drone ]
UAV
Unmanned Aerial Vehicles

From crop monitoring to medical deliveries. Zipline flies blood supplies across Africa and the U.S. by drone. But flight time is 20 to 40 minutes per charge. Every landing is lost time.

[ Photo: industrial robot outdoors ]
Industrial
Heavy Duty & Outdoor

Mining bots, farm robots, construction machines. They work in harsh, remote places far from any outlet. Heavy loads, nonstop schedules, zero infrastructure.

What we're building

The full charging infrastructure for autonomous fleets.

01

The Charger

A wireless charging station designed for autonomous docking. The robot drives up, aligns, and charges. No plugs, no cables, no human. Deployable indoors and outdoors.

[ Photo: charger station ]
02

The Retrofit Kit

A receiver and adapter kit that fits onto existing robots. No need to redesign the robot. We retrofit any fleet with wireless charging capability.

[ Photo: retrofit kit, receiver + adapter ]
03

Navigation Software

The robot needs to find the charger and dock precisely. Our navigation layer guides robots to the charging station autonomously, with centimeter-level alignment.

04

Orchestration Software

Fleet-level intelligence. Which robot charges when, where, and for how long. Optimizes uptime across the entire fleet so no robot is ever idle waiting to charge.
