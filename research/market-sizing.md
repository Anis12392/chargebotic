### Claim Verification: "30% Fleet Time Lost to Charging"

**VERDICT: SUPPORTED - This is a well-documented industry problem**

Evidence:
- **Nyobolt/industry data:** "Most mobile robots lose up to 3 hours per day to charging stops" -- in a 10-hour operation, that is 30%
- **Fleet oversizing:** Operators routinely oversize fleets by 20-30% just to compensate for charging downtime
- **Work-to-Charge Ratio:** Real-world data shows ~80% work / 20% charge split as a baseline (robots charge 22 min, run 98 min), but this worsens with fleet queuing and congestion
- **CaPow claims:** Eliminating downtime due to charging can cut fleet size by 25% or more
- **Tier-1 automotive case study:** Implementing continuous power avoided adding 20% more robots and cut automation-related losses by 50%

**This is a strong, defensible claim.** The 20-30% range is widely cited in industry. Use "up to 30%" for safety.

### Competitive Landscape Analysis

**Is there really no universal charging layer? CONFIRMED -- There is NOT.**

Evidence:
- Every major robot OEM ships its own proprietary charging station
- WiBotic is the closest to "universal" but operates primarily as an aftermarket wireless charging add-on
- CaPow is innovative (charging in motion) but focused on a single charging method, not a platform
- Nyobolt is a battery technology company, not a charging infrastructure company
- Industry sources confirm: "The industry needs standardized, easy-to-adopt charging systems that don't lock customers into one automation vendor or robotics platform"
- Standardization efforts exist (USB-C PD 3.1/3.2 for lower power; emerging wireless standards) but no single company has built the "Android of robot charging"

**Key gap Chargebotic can fill:**
- WiBotic = wireless hardware. CaPow = in-motion hardware. Nyobolt = battery chemistry.
- **Nobody is building the full-stack universal platform:** hardware-agnostic software orchestration + multi-protocol charging support + fleet energy management intelligence
- The "software orchestration" layer that manages energy across heterogeneous fleets does not exist as a standalone product
